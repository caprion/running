## Form Improvement Focus: Arm Swing Mechanics

**Issue Identified:** Static 90° arm hold with limited anterior-posterior swing (Jan 2026 gait analysis)

**Root Cause:**
- Arms held rigid at 90° angle rather than dynamic "drumming" motion
- Limited range of motion (front-back swing)
- Tension in shoulders creating energy waste
- Impacts efficiency, cadence, and breathing

**Correction Plan (Weeks 1-20):**
1. **Pre-run arm swing drill** - Every run, 2 minutes before starting
2. **During-run drills:**
   - Easy runs: Exaggerated arm swing bursts (3-4 x 30s)
   - Quality runs (Week 3+): Strides with arm focus (4-6 x 20s)
   - Long runs: Conscious check-ins every 5 minutes
3. **Post-run shoulder work** - Tue/Sun (shoulder blade squeezes, band pull-aparts)
4. **Strength additions** - Scapular push-ups added to Session B for shoulder stability

**Gait Analysis Schedule:**
- Week 1 (Jan 10): Baseline
- Week 5 (Feb 7): Early progress check
- Week 9 (Mar 7): Mid-base phase assessment
- Week 13 (Apr 4): Pre-10K taper
- Week 17 (May 2): Pre-HM specific phase

**Expected Benefits:**
- Reduced energy waste from upper body tension
- Improved cadence (arm-leg linkage)
- Better breathing (relaxed shoulders)
- More efficient race execution

**Full drill guide:** `resources/arm-swing-drills-guide.md`

## Contingency Rules

### Adjustment Protocols

| Scenario | Running Response | Strength Response |
|----------|------------------|-------------------|
| Miss 1 run in week | Continue plan, don't double up | Keep strength |
| Miss 2+ runs | Repeat week or reduce 20% | Keep strength |

| Travel week (cannot run) | 0km acceptable | 2 bodyweight sessions |

| Illness >5 days | Reset to previous phase 1 week | Reset to Wk 3 loads |
| Minor pain (<3/10) | Skip intervals, keep easy | Modify around pain |
| Pain >3 days or >5/10 | Stop running, medical eval | Upper body only |

### Minimum Viable Week (Floor)

| Condition | Running Minimum | Strength Minimum |
|-----------|-----------------|------------------|
| Normal week | 35-45 km (4-5 runs) | 2 full + 2 activation |
| Busy/travel week | 15-20 km (3×5-7km easy) | 1 bodyweight session |

### Key Decision Rules

- If in doubt, run easy instead of hard
- Never add volume/intensity to "make up" missed days
- 3 consecutive bad workouts = take 3 extra easy days
- Resting HR elevated >5 bpm for 3 days = reduce load 30%
- Any sharp pain = stop immediately
- Sleep <6 hours = convert next quality session to easy
- Race week: if anything feels off, default to conservative pacing

## Recovery Monitoring Thresholds

**Traffic Light System for Recovery Metrics:**

| Metric | 🟢 Green (Good) | 🟡 Yellow (Monitor) | 🔴 Red (Rest/Adjust) |
|--------|-----------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Resting HR | <62 bpm | 62-67 bpm | >67 bpm |
| RHR vs Baseline | Within 3 bpm | 3-5 bpm elevated | >5 bpm elevated |
| Sleep Duration | ≥7.5h | 6-7.5h | <6h |
| Deep Sleep % | ≥15% | 10-15% | <10% |
| Garmin Recovery Time | <24h | 24-48h | >48h |
| Training Status | Productive/Peaking | Maintaining | Unproductive/Detraining |
| Subjective Energy | 7-10/10 | 4-6/10 | 1-3/10 |

**Automated Response Protocol:**
- 🟢 All Green: Proceed with planned training
- 🟡 1-2 Yellow: Monitor, consider easier intensity
- 🟡 3+ Yellow: Convert quality session to easy run
- 🔴 Any Red: Mandatory easy day or rest; reassess next day

**Weekly Recovery Check (Sunday PM):**
1. Review week's RHR trend (dashboard)
2. Check sleep averages (target: ≥7.5h avg)
3. Note Garmin Training Status
4. Adjust next week's plan if needed

### Interpreting Garmin Training Status

Training Status is a lagging indicator based on VO2max trend and training load. Use context:

| Status | Meaning | Action |
|--------|---------|--------|
| **Productive** | VO2max trending up, load balanced | Continue current plan |
| **Peaking** | Fitness high, freshness building | Taper working, race ready |
| **Maintaining** | Stable fitness, no gains | May need more stimulus (volume or intensity) |
| **Recovery** | Low training load | Expected during recovery/deload weeks |
| **Unproductive** | High load but VO2max flat/down | Check recovery, may need rest |
| **Detraining** | Fitness declining | Expected in recovery phase post-race |
| **Overreaching** | Too much too fast | Reduce load, prioritize recovery |

**Context Matters:**
- **Post-race (1-2 weeks):** "Unproductive" or "Detraining" is NORMAL — don't panic
- **Recovery weeks:** Low training load = expected status drop
- **Look at 2-3 week trends**, not daily status
- **VO2max is the key metric** — status follows VO2max direction

**When to be concerned:**
- "Unproductive" for 3+ weeks during normal training
- RHR elevated + poor sleep + Unproductive = true overtraining risk

## Historical Context

### Volume Pattern Issue (2025)
- Jan-Mar: 30-45 km/week (motivated, building)
- Apr-Jul: 5-20 km/week (travel, family events — volume collapse)

## Summary

This is a 20-week campaign to take a 2:08 HM runner with demonstrated 5:33/km training capability to a sub-2:03 (ideally sub-2:00) HM through:

1. **Consistent volume** (35-50 km/week with minimum floor)
2. **Integrated strength** (2×/week, periodized with running phases)
3. **Progressive intensity** (tempo → intervals → race-specific long runs)
4. **10K tune-up race** for execution practice and goal calibration
5. **Conservative taper** with explicit deload weeks built in

The constraint is not fitness — it's accessing fitness through confident race execution.
